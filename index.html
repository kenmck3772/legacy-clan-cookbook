The Flamekeeper's Story

A Note from the Author

"My name is Kenneth Scott McKenzie. It’s a name that carries two Highland legacies. My mother is a McKenzie, her father Kenneth Candlish McKenzie, a name that whispers of the Isle of Lewis and the great Seaforth chiefs. My father is an Armstrong, a name that rings with the fierce, defiant spirit of the Border Reivers. I am the first of my generation to carry the McKenzie name forward, a conscious choice to tend the flame of a story that blends the visionary fire of the Mackenzies with the warrior heart of the Armstrongs. This project is not just a cookbook. It’s for the forgotten families, the blended bloodlines, and the quiet names that held the Highlands together. This is my legacy—and theirs."

Bonus Chapter: The Seer's Kitchen

"When the broth speaks, the wise listen." A tribute to the mystics, healers, and prophets of the Highlands—and the foods they revered. The Highland Seer, or *fiosaiche*, was not simply a fortune-teller. They were healers and herbalists who cooked with sacred intent, believing food had spiritual power to open the mind to second sight.

Bonus Chapter: Feasts of the Forbidden

A tribute to the defiant clans whose cuisine was shaped by proscription, persecution, and pride. They cooked when cooking was treason and shared food even when famine loomed. These were not banquets in castles—these were rebellion feasts in the woods, caves, and cattle sheds.

Bonus Chapter: Whisky & War Stories

"A dram before battle, a dram for the fallen, a dram for the road." Whisky wasn’t just a drink. It was a ritual, a currency, and a keeper of memory. Every clan had their own distilling traditions, secret stills, and ways of blessing the dram.
